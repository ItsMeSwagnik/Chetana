Understanding Depression

What is Depression?

Depression is more than just feeling sad or going through a rough patch. It's a serious mental health condition that affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.

Common Signs & Symptoms

  • Persistent sad, anxious, or "empty" mood
  • Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed
  • Fatigue and decreased energy
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Changes in sleep patterns
  • Changes in appetite or weight

Behavioral Activation

What is Behavioral Activation?

Behavioral Activation is a therapeutic approach that focuses on scheduling positive activities to improve mood and break the cycle of depression.

How It Works

When we're depressed, we often withdraw from activities we once enjoyed. This creates a negative cycle where inactivity leads to worse mood, which leads to more inactivity.

Breathing Exercise

4-7-8 Breathing Technique

This technique helps activate your body's relaxation response and reduce anxiety.

Ready to begin?

How to Practice:

  1. Inhale through your nose for 4 counts
  2. Hold your breath for 7 counts
  3. Exhale through your mouth for 8 counts
  4. Repeat 4-8 cycles

Benefits:

  • Reduces anxiety and stress
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Lowers heart rate
  • Promotes relaxation
