Understanding Depression

What is Depression?

Depression is more than just feeling sad or going through a rough patch. It's a serious mental health condition that affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.

Common Signs & Symptoms

  • Persistent sad, anxious, or "empty" mood
  • Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed
  • Fatigue and decreased energy
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Changes in sleep patterns
  • Changes in appetite or weight

Coping Strategies

üåÖ Daily Routine

Maintain a consistent daily schedule to provide structure and stability.

üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Physical Activity

Regular exercise can boost mood and energy levels naturally.

ü§ù Social Connection

Stay connected with friends and family, even when you don't feel like it.

üéØ Small Goals

Set achievable daily goals to build momentum and confidence.

When to Seek Help

If symptoms persist for more than two weeks or interfere with daily life, consider reaching out to a mental health professional.

Behavioral Activation

What is Behavioral Activation?

Behavioral Activation is a therapeutic approach that focuses on scheduling positive activities to improve mood and break the cycle of depression.

How It Works

When we're depressed, we often withdraw from activities we once enjoyed. This creates a negative cycle where inactivity leads to worse mood, which leads to more inactivity.

Breathing Exercise

4-7-8 Breathing Technique

This technique helps activate your body's relaxation response and reduce anxiety.

Ready to begin?

How to Practice:

  1. Inhale through your nose for 4 counts
  2. Hold your breath for 7 counts
  3. Exhale through your mouth for 8 counts
  4. Repeat 4-8 cycles

Benefits:

  • Reduces anxiety and stress
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Lowers heart rate
  • Promotes relaxation

Understanding Anxiety

            

                
            

        

        

            

                
What is Anxiety?

                
Anxiety is a natural response to stress, but when it becomes excessive or persistent, it can interfere with daily activities and quality of life.

                
                
Types of Anxiety Disorders

                

                    

                        
üå™Ô∏è Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

                        
Excessive worry about everyday situations and events.

                    

                    

                        
üò∞ Panic Disorder

                        
Sudden episodes of intense fear with physical symptoms.

                    

                    

                        
üë• Social Anxiety

                        
Fear of social situations and being judged by others.

                    

                    

                        
üï∑Ô∏è Specific Phobias

                        
Intense fear of specific objects or situations.

                    

                


                
Common Symptoms

                
    
                    
  • Excessive worry or fear
    • 
                    
  • Restlessness or feeling on edge
    • 
                    
  • Rapid heartbeat or palpitations
    • 
                    
  • Sweating or trembling
    • 
                    
  • Difficulty concentrating
    • 
                    
  • Sleep disturbances
    • 
                    
  • Muscle tension
    • 
                    
  • Avoidance of anxiety-provoking situations
    • 
                


                
Coping Strategies

                

                    

                        
üßò Deep Breathing

                        
Practice slow, deep breathing to activate the body's relaxation response.

                    

                    

                        
üéØ Grounding Techniques

                        
Use 5-4-3-2-1 technique: 5 things you see, 4 you touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.

                    

                    

                        
üí≠ Challenge Negative Thoughts

                        
Question anxious thoughts and replace them with more realistic ones.

                    

                    

                        
üìù Gradual Exposure

                        
Gradually face feared situations in small, manageable steps.

                    

                    

                        
üèÉ Regular Exercise

                        
Physical activity helps reduce anxiety and improve mood naturally.

                    

                    

                        
üò¥ Sleep Hygiene

                        
Maintain regular sleep schedule and create a calming bedtime routine.

                    

                


                

                    
When to Seek Professional Help

                    
Consider professional support if anxiety:

                    
    
                        
  • Interferes with work, school, or relationships
    • 
                        
  • Causes physical symptoms like panic attacks
    • 
                        
  • Leads to avoidance of important activities
    • 
                        
  • Persists for several weeks or months
    • 
                        
  • Includes thoughts of self-harm

Understanding Stress

            

                
            

        

        

            

                
What is Stress?

                
Stress is the body's natural response to challenges or demands. While some stress can be motivating, chronic stress can negatively impact physical and mental health.

                
                
Types of Stress

                

                    

                        
‚ö° Acute Stress

                        
Short-term stress from immediate pressures or demands. Usually manageable and temporary.

                    

                    

                        
üîÑ Episodic Acute Stress

                        
Frequent episodes of acute stress, often in people who take on too much.

                    

                    

                        
üìà Chronic Stress

                        
Long-term stress from ongoing situations like work pressure or relationship problems.

                    

                    

                        
‚ú® Eustress

                        
Positive stress that motivates and energizes, like starting a new job or getting married.

                    

                


                
Common Stress Symptoms

                

                    

                        
üß† Emotional Symptoms

                        
    
                            
  • Irritability or anger
    • 
                            
  • Anxiety or restlessness
    • 
                            
  • Feeling overwhelmed
    • 
                            
  • Low motivation
    • 
                            
  • Sadness or depression
    • 
                        

                    

                    

                        
üèÉ Physical Symptoms

                        
    
                            
  • Headaches
    • 
                            
  • Muscle tension
    • 
                            
  • Fatigue
    • 
                            
  • Sleep problems
    • 
                            
  • Digestive issues
    • 
                        

                    

                    

                        
ü§î Cognitive Symptoms

                        
    
                            
  • Racing thoughts
    • 
                            
  • Forgetfulness
    • 
                            
  • Difficulty concentrating
    • 
                            
  • Poor judgment
    • 
                            
  • Constant worrying
    • 
                        

                    

                


                
Healthy Coping Mechanisms

                

                    

                        
‚è∞ Time Management

                        
Prioritize tasks, set realistic goals, and break large projects into smaller steps.

                    

                    

                        
üßò Relaxation Techniques

                        
Practice meditation, progressive muscle relaxation, or yoga.

                    

                    

                        
ü§ù Social Support

                        
Connect with friends, family, or support groups. Don't isolate yourself.

                    

                    

                        
üèÉ Physical Activity

                        
Regular exercise releases endorphins and reduces stress hormones.

                    

                    

                        
üé® Hobbies & Interests

                        
Engage in activities you enjoy to provide mental breaks and fulfillment.

                    

                    

                        
üö´ Healthy Boundaries

                        
Learn to say no to excessive demands and protect your personal time.

                    

                


                
Unhealthy Coping Mechanisms to Avoid

                

                    

                        
üç∑ Substance Use

                        
Alcohol, drugs, or excessive caffeine can worsen stress and create dependency.

                    

                    

                        
üçî Emotional Eating

                        
Using food to cope can lead to health problems and guilt.

                    

                    

                        
üò¥ Avoidance

                        
Procrastination or avoiding responsibilities often increases stress.

                    

                    

                        
üò° Aggression

                        
Taking stress out on others damages relationships and doesn't solve problems.

                    

                


                

                    
When Stress Becomes a Problem

                    
Seek professional help if stress:

                    
    
                        
  • Significantly impacts your daily functioning
    • 
                        
  • Causes persistent physical symptoms
    • 
                        
  • Leads to substance abuse or other harmful behaviors
    • 
                        
  • Results in thoughts of self-harm
    • 
                        
  • Doesn't improve with self-care strategies
